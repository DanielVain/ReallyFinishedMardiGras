The Mardi Gras Fetival

The French Quarter of New Orleans, the largest city in the state of Louisiana in the southern United States, becomes a hotbed of unbridled parties and celebrations for two weeks a year. The famous carnival, the Mardi Gras (French For "Fat Tuesday"), held in many places around the world, reaches new heights in New Orleans of unparalleled beauty and joy. The wonderful New Orleans Carnival, first begun in 1857 by French exiles, is a global attraction for thousands of locals and tourists planning to arrive in the city many months in advance. "Fat Tuesday" (the beginning of the 40-day meat-eating fast until Easter) where two colorful carnival parades take place on St. Charles Street. A visit to the city during the carnival days guarantees the visitor a unique cultural experience.
